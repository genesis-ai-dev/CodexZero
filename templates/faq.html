Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about CodexZero and the translation process

General

What is CodexZero?

CodexZero is a tool for generating zero drafts of Bible translations using AI. It helps translators create initial drafts that preserve the target language's natural structure rather than imposing source language patterns.

How does CodexZero work?

CodexZero uses a three-step translation loop: (1) Generate back translations of your training materials to understand target language structure, (2) Use those patterns to translate new passages, and (3) Review and refine the results. This approach helps avoid imposing source language syntax onto target languages.

Is CodexZero free to use?

The platform itself is free. However, AI translation services have costs. We use batch processing to reduce these costs by 50% compared to real-time translation, making it more affordable for translation projects.

Back Translations

What are back translations?

Back translations are literal, word-for-word translations of your target language materials back into English. They help the AI understand the natural structure and patterns of your target language, preventing it from imposing English grammar onto the target language.

Why do back translations take so long?

We use batch processing to reduce costs by 50%. This means your translation requests are queued and processed together with others, which can take up to 24 hours. The trade-off is significant cost savings for large translation projects.

Can I leave the page while back translations are processing?

Yes! You can safely close your browser or navigate away from the page. Your job will continue processing in the background. Just return to your project page later to check the results.

What files can I upload for back translation?

You can upload eBible files or paste text directly. The system works best with existing translations, reference materials, or any text in your target language that demonstrates natural language patterns.

Language Rules & Context

What are language rules?

Language rules are custom instructions you provide to guide the AI translator. They can include grammar preferences, cultural considerations, vocabulary choices, or any specific guidance about how to translate for your target language and audience.

What kind of language rules should I add?

Examples include: "Use active voice instead of passive voice," "Avoid direct commands as they're considered rude," "Use 'start' instead of 'commence'," or "Use British spelling (colour, honour, etc.)." The more specific, the better.

Can I edit language rules after creating a project?

Yes! You can add, edit, or remove language rules at any time by clicking the "Edit Project" button on your project page.

Technical

Which AI model does CodexZero use?

We use Anthropic's Claude Sonnet 4, which is specifically designed for high-quality text processing and translation tasks.

Is my data secure?

Yes. Your projects and files are private to your account. Translation jobs are processed securely through Anthropic's API. Batch results are available for 29 days before being automatically deleted.

What happens if a batch job fails?

If a batch job fails or expires, you'll see an error message on your project page. You can simply restart the process. Failed jobs don't incur any costs.

How accurate are the translations?

CodexZero creates zero drafts - initial translations that require human review and refinement. The quality depends on your training materials and language rules. Always have qualified translators review and revise the output.
