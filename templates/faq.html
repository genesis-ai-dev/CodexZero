Frequently Asked Questions

Key information about using CodexZero for Bible translation

What is file pairing and why do I need it?

File pairing links two files that are line-by-line translations of each other (like English and Spanish versions of the same text). This is essential for fine-tuning custom AI models and providing translation examples. Upload both files to your project, then click the "Pair" button next to one file and select the matching file.

What is fine-tuning and should I use it?

Fine-tuning creates custom AI models trained on your specific translation pairs. This significantly improves translation quality for your target language and style. You need paired files with at least 50 examples (500+ recommended). Training takes 1-3 hours, and the system provides cost estimates before starting.

What file formats can I upload?

CodexZero supports .txt files (UTF-8 encoded), USFM files (.usfm, .sfm) from Paratext, and direct text pasting. The system automatically detects USFM format and provides specialized import tools. You can also import from the built-in eBible corpus.

How do translation instructions work?

Translation instructions are custom guidelines that help the AI understand your specific needs. Include guidance like "Use simple vocabulary for young adults," "Avoid passive voice," or "Maintain formal tone in prayer passages." The more specific you are, the better the AI will follow your style preferences.

Is CodexZero really free?

Yes, CodexZero is completely free to use! The platform provides AI translation services at no cost. If you'd like to support the project, you can contact us about making a donation.
